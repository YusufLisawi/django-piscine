Brief Introduction to Django

Django is a high-level Python web framework that follows the Model-View-Controller (MVC) architectural pattern. It provides a robust set of tools and features for building web applications quickly and efficiently.

History of Django

Django was created in 2003 by Adrian Holovaty and Simon Willison while working at the Lawrence Journal-World newspaper in Kansas, USA. They needed a framework to build web applications rapidly and decided to create their own.

Django was released as an open-source project in July 2005, and it gained popularity quickly due to its simplicity, scalability, and emphasis on best practices.

In 2008, the Django Software Foundation (DSF) was formed to support and maintain the development of Django. The DSF ensures the long-term stability and growth of the framework.

Over the years, Django has evolved and introduced numerous features, including an Object-Relational Mapping (ORM) layer, a powerful template engine, built-in authentication and authorization, form handling, internationalization support, and more.

Django has been widely adopted by developers and is used by many popular websites and organizations, including Instagram, Pinterest, Mozilla, and The Washington Post.

The latest stable version of Django, as of September 2021, is Django 3.2, with regular updates and improvements being released by the Django community.
